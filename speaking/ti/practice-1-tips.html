Day 1 Homework — Technology and Learning

🎤 Take an Interview 攻略ポイントStrategy tips
1 45秒の構成：主張→理由→具体例→まとめ45-second structure: claim → reason → example → wrap-up
45秒を以下のように配分する。全パートを言い切ることが最重要。Allocate the 45 seconds as below. Most important: finish every part.
主張 5秒Claim 5s
理由 15秒Reason 15s
具体例 15秒Example 15s
まとめ 10秒Wrap-up 10s
主張： "I definitely think that..." （最初の1文で立場を明確に）
理由： 1つの理由を深く掘る（2つ浅くより1つ深くが高得点）
具体例： 自分の経験を話す（"When I was..." / "For example, last year..."）
まとめ： "So overall..." / "That's why I believe..." で締めるClaim: "I definitely think that..." (state your position in one sentence)
Reason: Dig deep into one reason (one deep reason scores higher than two shallow)
Example: Tell a personal story ("When I was..." / "For example, last year...")
Wrap-up: Close with "So overall..." or "That's why I believe..."
2 沈黙を避けるフレーズ集Phrases to avoid silence
考え中でも黙らない。以下のフレーズで繋ぐ。Don't go silent while thinking. Use these fillers to bridge.
考え中Thinking
Let me think about that...
That's a great question...
始めるStarting
Well, in my opinion...
I would say that...
例を出すGiving an example
For instance...
To give you an example...
まとめWrapping up
So overall...
That's why I believe...
3 理由は「2つ浅く」より「1つ深く」One deep reason beats two shallow ones
理由を2つ挙げようとすると、どちらも中途半端になりがち。1つの理由を深く掘り下げて、具体例まで到達するのが高得点パターン。Trying to give two reasons usually leaves both half-baked. The high-scoring pattern: dig into one reason and reach a concrete example.
✗ NG： "First, technology is useful. Second, it saves time." → 2つとも浅い。具体例がない。✗ Bad: "First, technology is useful. Second, it saves time." → both shallow, no example.
✓ OK： "The main reason is that technology makes studying more efficient. For example, when I used a vocabulary app last semester, I was able to review words during my commute, which saved me about 30 minutes every day..." → 1つの理由を具体例で裏付け。✓ Good: "The main reason is that technology makes studying more efficient. For example, when I used a vocabulary app last semester, I was able to review words during my commute, which saved me about 30 minutes every day..." → one reason backed by an example.
4 具体例は自分の経験で（作り話でOK）Use personal experience for examples (made-up is fine)
自分の経験を話すと、自然に流暢に話せる。実体験でなくても構わない — 大事なのは流暢さと構成。Speaking from personal experience makes you naturally fluent. It doesn't have to be true — what matters is fluency and structure.
便利な出だし：Useful openers:
When I was in high school...
Last year, I had an experience where...
For example, my friend once told me that...
📝 各質問の分析 & 回答のコツPer-question analysis & tips
Q1 Factual / Personal（経験）Factual / Personal (experience)
"What kinds of technology do you use most often for studying or learning new things?"
何を聞かれている？ 自分が使っている学習ツール（具体名）。
答え方のコツ： 具体的なツール名を1〜2個挙げて、どう使っているか簡潔に。Q1は事実を答えるだけなので、構えすぎなくてOK。
模範構成： "I mostly use [ツール名] for [用途]. I also sometimes use [ツール名] when I need to [用途]."What's being asked? What learning tools you use (specific names).
How to answer: Name 1–2 concrete tools and briefly say how you use them. Q1 is just stating facts — no need to over-prepare.
Template: "I mostly use [tool] for [purpose]. I also sometimes use [tool] when I need to [purpose]."
Q2 Behavioral / Personal（行動と理由）Behavioral / Personal (action + reason)
"How has technology changed the way you personally study or learn, and do you think these changes have been mostly positive?"
何を聞かれている？ テクノロジーによる学習の変化と、それが良いかどうか。
答え方のコツ： 2部構成。① Before/After で変化を説明 → ② positive/negative の判断＋理由。
模範構成： "Before, I used to [旧方法]. But now, [新方法]. I think this has been mostly positive because [理由]."What's being asked? How tech has changed your learning, and whether that's good.
How to answer: Two parts. ① Describe the change with Before/After → ② state positive/negative + reason.
Template: "Before, I used to [old method]. But now, [new method]. I think this has been mostly positive because [reason]."
Q3 Opinion / Argue（意見＋根拠）Opinion / Argue (position + support)
"Some educators believe that handwriting notes is more effective for learning than typing on a laptop. Do you agree that handwriting is better for learning? Why or why not?"
何を聞かれている？ 手書きとタイピング、どちらが学習に効果的か。
答え方のコツ： まず立場を1文で明確に（agree / disagree）。次に理由を1つ深く。最後に自分の経験で裏付け。
45秒構成例：
[5秒] "I agree that handwriting is more effective for learning."
[15秒] "The main reason is that when you write by hand, you have to process the information more carefully because you can't write as fast as you type. This forces you to summarize and think about what you're writing."
[15秒] "For example, when I take notes by hand in class, I tend to remember the key points much better than when I just type everything word for word on my laptop."
[10秒] "So overall, I believe handwriting helps you engage with the material more deeply."What's being asked? Which is more effective for learning — handwriting or typing.
How to answer: State your position clearly in one sentence (agree / disagree). Then dig into one reason. Finish with personal experience.
45-second example:
[5s] "I agree that handwriting is more effective for learning."
[15s] "The main reason is that when you write by hand, you have to process the information more carefully because you can't write as fast as you type. This forces you to summarize and think about what you're writing."
[15s] "For example, when I take notes by hand in class, I tend to remember the key points much better than when I just type everything word for word on my laptop."
[10s] "So overall, I believe handwriting helps you engage with the material more deeply."
Q4 Opinion / Broader Issue（社会的テーマ）Opinion / Broader Issue (societal theme)
"Do you think AI tools in education will mostly help students learn better, or could they create new problems? Why?"
何を聞かれている？ AI教育ツールのメリットとリスクについての意見。
答え方のコツ： Q4は最も広いテーマ。片方の立場に絞るのが安全（"both" と言うと浅くなりがち）。具体例まで到達すること。
注意： Q4で時間切れになる人が多い。まとめの "So overall..." は短くてOK。具体例を途中で打ち切ってでも、まとめを言い切ること！What's being asked? Your opinion on AI tools in education — benefits and risks.
How to answer: Q4 has the broadest topic. Pick one side — saying "both" tends to make answers shallow. Make sure to reach a concrete example.
Caution: Many test-takers run out of time here. The wrap-up "So overall..." can be very short. If needed, cut the example short to make sure you finish the wrap-up!
避けたいパターン：
✗ "I think AI has both good and bad sides..." → 両方に触れて浅くなる
✗ 理由を2つ挙げて具体例まで行けない → 時間切れPatterns to avoid:
✗ "I think AI has both good and bad sides..." → covering both sides leaves you shallow
✗ Listing two reasons and never reaching an example → you'll time out
🏋️ 自主練習の方法🏋️ Self-practice routine
Step 1: スマホのタイマーを45秒にセットする
Step 2: 下の「各質問の分析」セクションからQ1〜Q4のどれかを選び、質問を声に出して読む（本番では音声で流れる部分）
Step 3: 読み終わったらすぐタイマースタート → 45秒以内に「主張→理由→具体例→まとめ」を言い切る
Step 4: 録音して聞き返す → 沈黙がないか、まとめまで到達しているかチェック

💡 本番では質問は音声のみ（画面にテキストは表示されません）。聞いた瞬間に回答を組み立てる練習が重要！

目標： 1日2〜3回のルーティンを体に覚えさせる。完璧な英語でなくてOK — 止まらず話し続けることが最優先！Step 1: Set your phone timer to 45 seconds.
Step 2: Pick any of Q1–Q4 from the analysis section above and read the question aloud (this is the audio portion on the test).
Step 3: Start the timer immediately → finish "claim → reason → example → wrap-up" within 45 seconds.
Step 4: Record and listen back → check for silences and whether you reached the wrap-up.

💡 On the real test, questions are audio only (no on-screen text). Practice composing your answer the moment you hear the question!

Goal: Build the routine 2–3 times a day until your body knows it. Your English doesn't have to be perfect — the priority is to keep talking without stopping!
