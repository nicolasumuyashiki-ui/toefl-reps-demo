Discussion Board
Dr. Nakamura
As cities continue to grow, urban green spaces such as parks and community gardens are increasingly being replaced by housing developments and commercial buildings. Some argue that preserving these green areas is essential for the well-being of residents, while others believe that new construction is necessary to meet the demands of a growing population. What do you think? Should cities prioritize preserving urban green spaces, or should they allow more construction to address housing needs? Why?
Priya
I believe cities should prioritize preserving green spaces. Parks and gardens provide mental health benefits by giving people a place to relax and escape the stress of urban life. Studies show that access to nature reduces anxiety and depression. Without these spaces, residents would have fewer options for outdoor recreation and social interaction.
Marcus
I understand Priya's point, but I think housing should take priority. In many cities, rent is extremely high because there simply aren't enough homes. Building more affordable housing would directly improve people's quality of life. We can always find creative solutions for green space, like rooftop gardens or vertical parks.
