📋 問題の確認
Scenario: You are a university student, and the campus newspaper recently published an article about a new study abroad program. However, the article contains several factual errors about the program's application deadline, eligibility requirements, and available destinations. As someone who has already applied to the program, you are familiar with the correct details.

Write an email to the editor of the campus newspaper. In your email:

  • Point 1 Compliment the newspaper for covering the study abroad program.
  • Point 2 Identify the specific errors in the article and provide the correct information.
  • Point 3 Suggest a way to ensure future articles about university programs are more accurate.
✅ 模範回答（約110語）
Dear Editor,

1 I wanted to let you know that I really appreciated the recent article about the new study abroad program. It's great to see the newspaper covering opportunities that are important to students.

2 However, I noticed a few errors in the article. The application deadline was listed as March 15, but it is actually April 1. Also, the article mentioned that only juniors and seniors are eligible, but sophomores can apply as well. Finally, the article said the program is limited to Europe, but it also includes destinations in Asia.

3 To avoid similar issues in the future, I would suggest reaching out directly to the program coordinator before publishing articles about university programs. This way, you can confirm the details and provide accurate information to readers.

Thank you for your time.
Best regards

⚠️ よくあるミス
✗ ポイント見落とし

3つのうち1つを書き忘れる → 大減点。書き終わったら必ず再確認

✗ 冗長な前置き

"I hope this email finds you well..." は不要。すぐに目的を述べる

✗ カジュアルすぎる表現

"Hey Prof!" → "Dear Professor" に。メールの適切なトーンを意識

✗ 書きすぎ

120語を超えても加点なし。簡潔に3ポイントを網羅することが重要
