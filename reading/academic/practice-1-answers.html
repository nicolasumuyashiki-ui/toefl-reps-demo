💡 Read an Academic Passage 攻略ポイント
1 言い換え（パラフレーズ）を見抜く
正解は必ず本文の「言い換え」。本文と同じ単語を使う選択肢はむしろ疑うこと。
言い換えの例：
demonstrated → showed
significant → important
proposed → suggested
fail to help → do not offer assistance
今回の例（Q16）： 本文 "prompted...to investigate why people sometimes fail to help" → 選択肢C "revealed why people often do not help" — 言い換えの典型！
2 構造を理解する
最初の文 → トピック・主張
中間 → 詳細・具体例・データ
最後の文 → 結論・まとめ

Main Idea問題は最初と最後だけで解けることが多い。
今回の例： 冒頭でGenovese事件 → 研究の紹介、中間で実験の詳細、最後で「社会心理学への影響」→ 全体を貫くテーマは「傍観者効果の研究」
3 推論の根拠を確認する
自分の知識や意見を混ぜない。「本文に書いてあること」だけが根拠。
最も多いミス：
✓ costs reduced → financially beneficial（本文の情報から推論可能）
✗ costs reduced → popular（根拠なし — 自分の想像を混ぜている）
The Bystander Effect Psychology
Question 16 — Main Idea
What is the passage mainly about?
Correct: C Your answer: —
A How a famous crime in New York City led to changes in police response procedures
B How social psychologists developed new treatments for anxiety in group settings
C How research into the bystander effect revealed why people often do not help in emergencies ✓
D How public safety programs have reduced violent crime rates in large cities
解法：構造で解く！
冒頭 → Genovese事件が研究のきっかけ。中間 → 実験と2つのメカニズム。結末 → 社会心理学への影響。全体を貫くのは「傍観者効果の研究」= C。

A は冒頭の事件だけに焦点を当てており、"police procedures"は本文にない（関連するが未言及の引っかけ）。B は anxiety treatments が未言及。D は最終文の "public safety training" を拡大解釈している。
パラフレーズ発見： 本文 "investigate why people sometimes fail to help" → C "revealed why people often do not help" — fail to help = do not help
Question 17 — Factual Information
According to the passage, what did Darley and Latané's intercom experiment show?
Correct: A Your answer: —
A Participants were more likely to seek help when they believed they were alone. ✓
B Participants responded faster when they knew other people were also listening.
C Participants who heard the seizure ignored it regardless of group size.
D Participants in larger groups reported the emergency within a few seconds.
Explanation: 本文 "believed they were the only listener, 85 percent sought help" vs "thought others were also listening, only 31 percent responded" → 一人の方が助けを求めやすい = A。

B は因果関係の逆転 — 他者がいると反応率が下がる。C は85%のデータと矛盾。D も31%のデータと矛盾。
Question 18 — Vocabulary in Context
The word "proposed" in paragraph 3 is closest in meaning to
Correct: C Your answer: —
A demanded
B imagined
C suggested ✓
D questioned
Explanation: 文脈では研究者が説明を「提案した」→ suggested が最も近い。demanded は強すぎる（研究者は主張を強制しない）。imagined は「想像した」で事実に基づく提案ではない。questioned は逆の意味（疑問を呈する）。

💡 Vocabulary in Context のコツ： 文脈に当てはめて意味が通るかチェック。"The researchers suggested that two mechanisms..." — 自然に読める！
Question 19 — Inference
Based on the passage, what can be inferred about a person who sees other bystanders remaining calm during an emergency?
Correct: B Your answer: —
A The person will likely feel greater personal responsibility to act immediately.
B The person may decide that the situation does not actually require assistance. ✓
C The person will almost certainly contact the authorities within a short time.
D The person is likely to have received public safety training at some earlier point.
推論の根拠を本文で確認：
本文 "pluralistic ignorance, in which people look at the reactions of others to decide whether the situation is truly an emergency. If no one else seems alarmed, individuals tend to conclude that help is not needed." → 他者が冷静 = 緊急ではないと判断 = B。

A は diffusion of responsibility と逆（集団→個人の責任感が下がる）。C は研究の予測と逆。D は本文に根拠なし。
推論問題の鉄則： 本文に書いてあることだけが根拠。「常識的にそうだろう」は通用しない！
Question 20 — Sentence Insertion
Sentence: "In other words, the presence of a group can reduce each individual's sense of obligation."
Correct: B Your answer: —
Position B が正解 — "The first is diffusion of responsibility, where each person assumes someone else will take action." の直後。

挿入文の冒頭 "In other words" は「言い換え」のシグナルワード。直前の文で述べた「責任の拡散」を別の表現で言い直している。

他の位置がダメな理由：
A — 1つ目のメカニズムが紹介される前なので、言い換える対象がない。
C — 2つ目のメカニズム（pluralistic ignorance）への転換を壊す。
D — 結論の後で唐突。段落の流れが途切れる。
🎯 鉄則まとめ
1. 正解は必ず本文の「言い換え」。同じ単語を使う選択肢は引っかけの可能性大。
2. 推論問題でも「本文に書いてあること」だけが根拠。自分の知識を混ぜない。
3. Main Idea は最初の文と最後の文で解ける。中間の詳細に引きずられない。
