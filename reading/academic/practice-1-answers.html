💡 Read an Academic Passage 攻略ポイントStrategy tips
1 言い換え（パラフレーズ）を見抜くSpot the paraphrase
正解は必ず本文の「言い換え」。本文と同じ単語を使う選択肢はむしろ疑うこと。The correct answer is always a paraphrase of the passage. Be suspicious of options that reuse the exact wording.
言い換えの例：Examples:
demonstrated → showed
significant → important
proposed → suggested
fail to help → do not offer assistance
今回の例（Q1）： 本文 "prompted...to investigate why people sometimes fail to help" → 選択肢C "revealed why people often do not help" — 言い換えの典型！This question (Q1): Passage "prompted...to investigate why people sometimes fail to help" → Option C "revealed why people often do not help" — a textbook paraphrase!
2 構造を理解するUnderstand the structure
最初の文 → トピック・主張
中間 → 詳細・具体例・データ
最後の文 → 結論・まとめ

Main Idea問題は最初と最後だけで解けることが多い。First sentence → topic / main claim
Middle → details / examples / data
Last sentence → conclusion / summary

Main Idea questions can often be solved using only the first and last sentences.
今回の例： 冒頭でGenovese事件 → 研究の紹介、中間で実験の詳細、最後で「社会心理学への影響」→ 全体を貫くテーマは「傍観者効果の研究」This passage: Opens with the Genovese case → introduces the research, middle covers experiments, ends with "impact on social psychology" → the throughline is "research on the bystander effect."
3 推論の根拠を確認するCheck the basis for inferences
自分の知識や意見を混ぜない。「本文に書いてあること」だけが根拠。Don't mix in your own knowledge or opinion. Only what the passage says counts as evidence.
最も多いミス：
✓ costs reduced → financially beneficial（本文の情報から推論可能）
✗ costs reduced → popular（根拠なし — 自分の想像を混ぜている）The most common mistake:
✓ costs reduced → financially beneficial (inferable from passage info)
✗ costs reduced → popular (no support — mixing in your imagination)
The Bystander Effect Psychology
Question 1 — Main Idea
What is the passage mainly about?
正解Correct: C あなたの解答:Your answer: —
A How a famous crime in New York City led to changes in police response procedures
B How social psychologists developed new treatments for anxiety in group settings
C How research into the bystander effect revealed why people often do not help in emergencies ✓
D How public safety programs have reduced violent crime rates in large cities
解法：構造で解く！
冒頭 → Genovese事件が研究のきっかけ。中間 → 実験と2つのメカニズム。結末 → 社会心理学への影響。全体を貫くのは「傍観者効果の研究」= C。

A は冒頭の事件だけに焦点を当てており、"police procedures"は本文にない（関連するが未言及の引っかけ）。B は anxiety treatments が未言及。D は最終文の "public safety training" を拡大解釈している。Strategy: solve via structure.
Opening → the Genovese case sparks the research. Middle → experiments and two mechanisms. End → impact on social psychology. The throughline is "research on the bystander effect" = C.

A focuses only on the opening case; "police procedures" is not in the passage (a related-but-unmentioned trap). B never mentions anxiety treatments. D over-extends the final sentence's "public safety training."

Question 2 — Sentence Insertion Harder Module
Sentence: "In other words, the presence of a group can reduce each individual's sense of obligation."
正解Correct: B あなたの解答:Your answer: —
Position B が正解 — "The first is diffusion of responsibility, where each person assumes someone else will take action." の直後。

挿入文の冒頭 "In other words" は「言い換え」のシグナルワード。直前の文で述べた「責任の拡散」を別の表現で言い直している。

他の位置がダメな理由：
A — 1つ目のメカニズムが紹介される前なので、言い換える対象がない。
C — 2つ目のメカニズム（pluralistic ignorance）への転換を壊す。
D — 結論の後で唐突。段落の流れが途切れる。Position B is correct — directly after "The first is diffusion of responsibility, where each person assumes someone else will take action."

The insertion sentence opens with "In other words", a paraphrase signal. It restates the "diffusion of responsibility" idea just introduced in different words.

Why the other positions fail:
A — Comes before the first mechanism is introduced, so there's nothing to paraphrase.
C — Breaks the transition into the second mechanism (pluralistic ignorance).
D — Abrupt placement after the conclusion; disrupts paragraph flow.
🎯 鉄則まとめ🎯 Key takeaways
1. 正解は必ず本文の「言い換え」。同じ単語を使う選択肢は引っかけの可能性大。
2. 推論問題でも「本文に書いてあること」だけが根拠。自分の知識を混ぜない。
3. Main Idea は最初の文と最後の文で解ける。中間の詳細に引きずられない。1. The correct answer is always a paraphrase. Options that reuse exact wording are usually traps.
2. Even on inference questions, only what's in the passage is evidence. Don't mix in outside knowledge.
3. Main Idea questions can be solved with the first and last sentences. Don't get distracted by middle details.
