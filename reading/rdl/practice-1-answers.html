💡 Read in Daily Life 攻略ポイントStrategy tips
1 冒頭のキーワードから目的を判別するIdentify purpose from the opening keywords
最初の1〜2文で文書の目的が分かります。パターンを覚えましょう：The first one or two sentences reveal the document's purpose. Memorize the patterns:

通知：Notification:We are pleased to inform...
依頼：Request:Could you... / I would appreciate if...
確認：Confirmation:I'm writing to confirm...
謝罪：Apology:I apologize for... / Unfortunately...
招待：Invitation:You are invited to... / Join us for...
例： メール冒頭 "Starting December 1, the main entrance will require..." → 変更の通知。目的問題は冒頭に答えがある！Example: An email opening with "Starting December 1, the main entrance will require..." → notification of a change. Purpose questions are answered in the opening!
2 NOT / EXCEPT 問題は消去法で解くSolve NOT / EXCEPT questions by elimination
手順：
① 設問の NOT / EXCEPT に丸をつける
② 4つの選択肢を本文と1つずつ照合
③ 本文に書いてある3つを消去 → 残り1つが正解Procedure:
① Circle NOT / EXCEPT in the question stem
② Check each of the four options against the passage
③ Eliminate the three that appear in the passage → the remaining one is correct
引っかけパターン：
✗ 本文の単語が出てくるが答えではない
✗ 部分的に正しいが全体として不正確
✗ 本文の逆を言っているCommon traps:
✗ Uses words from the passage but isn't the answer
✗ Partially correct but overall inaccurate
✗ States the opposite of the passage
3 イディオム・口語表現に慣れておくGet comfortable with idioms and casual expressions
テキストメッセージやSNSなどカジュアルな文書が出題されます。日常的なイディオムが問われることがあります。Text messages, SNS posts, and other casual documents appear here. Everyday idioms are often tested.
今回の例（Q1）： "I'm on the fence" = 「まだ決めていない」。文字通りの意味ではなく慣用的な意味が正解。This question (Q1): "I'm on the fence" = "haven't decided yet." The idiomatic meaning, not the literal one, is correct.
Passage 1 Text Messages
Question 1 — Vocabulary in Context
What does the sender mean by "I'm on the fence"?
正解Correct: B あなたの解答:Your answer: —
A She is sitting outside on a fence.
B She has not decided yet. ✓
C She is worried about the weather.
D She does not want to go at all.
Explanation: "on the fence" = undecided。直後に "I have homework" と迷う理由が続き、最終的に行くことに同意している。A は文字通りの意味に飛びつく引っかけ。C は本文に根拠なし。D は強すぎる — 実際には同意している。Explanation: "on the fence" = undecided. The next message gives the reason ("I have homework") and ultimately the sender agrees to go. A is a trap that leaps at the literal meaning. C has no support in the passage. D is too strong — in fact she agrees.
Question 2 — Detail
According to the messages, what time will the sender meet Kate?
正解Correct: C あなたの解答:Your answer: —
A 9:12 AM
B 9:18 AM
C 8:30 AM ✓
D 11:00 AM
Explanation: "OK fine. Bus stop at 8:30?" が待ち合わせ時刻を提案している箇所。A・B はメッセージの送信時刻（タイムスタンプ）の引っかけ。D は "be back by 11" — 帰着予定時刻なので集合時刻ではない。本文の at 8:30 を見つけられるかが鍵。Explanation: "OK fine. Bus stop at 8:30?" sets the meeting time. A and B are decoys taken from the message timestamps themselves. D refers to "be back by 11" — the return time, not the meet-up. Pinpoint at 8:30 in the passage.
Passage 2 Email
Question 3 — Main Purpose
What is the main purpose of this email?
正解Correct: B あなたの解答:Your answer: —
A To announce extended hours at the Café Annex
B To inform library users of a new silent-floor policy ✓
C To request student feedback on library design
D To advertise new study furniture in the Student Union
Explanation: 件名 "New Quiet Floor Policy" と冒頭段落 "the third and fourth floors ... will be designated silent study zones" が中心メッセージ。A は café annex への言及はあるが付随情報で主目的ではない。C・D は本文に根拠なし。主目的を聞かれたら 件名と第1段落を必ず確認する。Explanation: The subject line "New Quiet Floor Policy" and the first paragraph ("the third and fourth floors ... will be designated silent study zones") set the central message. A mentions the Café Annex but only as a side detail, not the main purpose. C and D have no support in the email. For "main purpose" questions, always check the subject line and first paragraph first.
Question 4 — Inference
What can be inferred about library users who repeatedly trigger the noise sensors?
正解Correct: B あなたの解答:Your answer: —
A They will receive a written warning by mail.
B They may temporarily lose access to the library. ✓
C They will be reported to academic services.
D They must attend a workshop on library etiquette.
Explanation: 本文に "Repeated alerts may result in a temporary loss of library access" と明示。B はその直接の言い換え。"may result in" の "may" は可能性なので、選択肢 B の "may" がそのままマッチ。A・C・D は本文に書かれていないので推測の根拠なし。推測問題でも、必ず本文に手がかりがあるのがポイント。Explanation: The email states, "Repeated alerts may result in a temporary loss of library access." B paraphrases this directly, preserving the "may" (possibility) hedge. A, C, and D have no textual support — so they're not inferences but inventions. Even "inference" questions must be anchored in the passage.
